Memorization Strategies

Science-backed techniques
🃏
Spaced Repetition (Leitner Box)
Review topics at increasing intervals — Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, Day 30. Use Anki or physical cards. This fights the forgetting curve and cements long-term memory.
🔬 Science-backed #1
🗺️
Mind Mapping
Draw radial maps connecting concepts. For eg: Constitution → Articles → Amendments → Case Laws. Visual networks are recalled 60% better than linear notes.
Visual Learner
🧑‍🏫
Feynman Technique
Explain any topic out loud as if teaching a 10-year-old. Where you stumble = what you don't know. Go back, re-read, simplify, repeat.
Deep Understanding
🔤
Mnemonics & Acronyms
Create memorable codes: "DPSP — Directive Principles State Policy" → "Don't Panic, Study Properly." Make them funny or personal — bizarre mnemonics stick best.
Quick Recall
⚡
Active Recall Testing
Close your notes and write down everything you remember. Then check. This retrieval practice is proven to be 3x more effective than re-reading alone.
High Impact
📖
The 3R Method
Read → Recite (without looking) → Review (compare). Apply to every chapter. Ideal for static subjects like Polity, History, and Anthropology theory.
Structured Study
🎭
Story / Palace Method
Link facts to a vivid story or place them in a mental walk through your house. Used by memory champions. Great for timelines, battles, constitutional articles, and tribal studies.
